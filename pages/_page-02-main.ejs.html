Campaign Finance

In lead-up to primary, Minnesota's nationally-watched congressional races bring in millions

By C.J. Sinner and Alan Palazzolo • Star Tribune

  • GOVERNOR
  • U.S. SENATE
  • U.S. HOUSE

No state is home to more toss-up congressional races than Minnesota, according to political experts, and fundraising in the state's four most competitive districts is already off to a blistering start. Districts 1, 2, 3 and 8 in particular are expected to be among the nation's most competitive — and expensive — races come November.

Figures below reflect reports filed since Jan. 1, 2017. The next quarterly filing deadline is October 15, 2018.

Third District hits $6M mark

July 17, 2018

The Third District, which encompasses the Hennepin County suburbs, is on pace to handily surpass the $7 million raised there in 2016. Meanwhile, fundraising in the suddenly-open Fifth is heating up, with Ilhan Omar emerging with a slight lead. In the Eighth, which includes Duluth and the Iron Range, Joseph Radinovich and Jason Metsa appear to be pulling away from the primary pack.

Spending close in First, Second, Third

July 17, 2018

In the tight southern Minnesota and Hennepin County suburb races, spending has been neck-and-neck. Democrats Angie Craig and Dean Phillips have outspent their incumbent opponents so far, but in the open First, Jim Hagedorn has spent about $50,000 more than Democrat Daniel Feehan.
